About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Biostatistics at Yale University, working with Prof. Yize Zhao. My research develops Bayesian hierarchical and meta‑regression models, graphical and network‑based modeling for brain connectivity, and scalable computation for complex, high‑dimensional biomedical data.

Latest work Application materials Collaborate
Bayesian computation Network meta‑regression Neuroimaging High‑dimensional inference

Highlights

  • Developing a Bayesian model for tau propagation on individualized functional connectivity (manuscript in prep.).
  • Multimodal ABCD & HCP analyses link functional–structural gradient coupling with development, behavior, and genetics (under revision).
  • Bayesian network meta‑regression for multivariate aggregate responses with partially/fully missing within‑treatment covariances (revise & resubmit, Biometrics).
Open to collaborations Mentoring MS/PhD students

Research Interests

Bayesian hierarchical & meta‑regression modeling; network/graphical modeling for brain connectivity; Bayesian computation & inference; statistical methods for complex, high‑dimensional biomedical data.

Current Projects

  • Functional–structural gradient coupling in development and aging; behavioral & genetic correlates.
  • Genetics → structure → function: mediation of genetic effects by structural connectivity on functional networks.
  • Tau propagation on individualized functional connectivity with subtype heterogeneity and sequential dynamics.

For Prospective Students

I enjoy mentoring on Bayesian modeling, reproducible pipelines, and neuroimaging statistics. If you’re a prospective graduate student interested in these topics, feel free to reach out with a short note about your background and interests.
